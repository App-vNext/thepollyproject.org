A few days ago I sat down (virtually) with Bryan Hogan of the [NoDogmaPodcast](https://web.archive.org/web/20250215134057/http://nodogmapodcast.bryanhogan.net/) to record a podcast on an [Introduction to Polly](https://web.archive.org/web/20250215134057/http://nodogmapodcast.bryanhogan.net/71-dylan-reisenberger-the-polly-project/).

In the podcast we talk about common resilience patterns, why you need them, and how you might use them - starting with well-known patterns such as Retry and Circuit-Breaker, and moving on to some of the more recent policies [added in v5.0](https://web.archive.org/web/20250215134057/http://www.thepollyproject.org/2016/10/25/polly-5-0-a-wider-resilience-framework/) by App-vNext, such as [Bulkhead Isolation](https://web.archive.org/web/20250215134057/https://github.com/App-vNext/Polly/wiki/Bulkhead).

Bryan has interviewed [a host of interesting speakers over recent years](https://web.archive.org/web/20250215134057/http://nodogmapodcast.bryanhogan.net/), including the likes of Jon Skeet, Scott Allen, Bill Wagner and Julie Lerman. Head on over to [NoDogmaPodcast](https://web.archive.org/web/20250215134057/http://nodogmapodcast.bryanhogan.net/71-dylan-reisenberger-the-polly-project/) to check it out.
